Melissodes

From "The melissodes project"

The genus Melissodes Latreille is a common group of new-worldNorth, South, and Centeral America long-horned bees in the tribe Eucerini (Apinae). The antenna of the males often appear to be quite long in comparison to their body lengths, giving them the name "long-horned bees". However, there are a few exceptions of bee species in the subgenus Melissodes that have varying antenna lengths. Characteristcs of the two sexes commonly differ in appearance, especially in antenna length and scopialElongated specialized hairs, often found on the hind legs, used for the distribution of pollen hair density (LaBerge, 1956). Melissodes is the second largest genus in Eucerini the tribe with 129 described bees and 5 current subgenera. The majority of the bees live in the United States and Mexico, except all species of Ecplectica are native to South America (Wright, 2016). Melissodes bees are shown to have some importance in agricultural pollination, pollinating crops such as cotton (LaBerge, 1956), alfalfa (LaBerge, 1956), muskmelons (Winfree, et al., 2007), watermelon (Campbell et al., 2018; Winfree, et al., 2007), conola (O'Brien & Arathi, 2018), and coffee (Ngo et al., 2013). Given the genus has so many species and is prevalent, it is reasonable to assume that they play an important role pollinating many other plants in nature as well.
